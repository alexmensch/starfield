Welcome to Starfield

Starfield is an interactive 3D map of — real stars. Each star is rendered at the colour and apparent brightness you would actually see from the camera's current position.

Drag to orbit, scroll to zoom, and hover over a star to see its name. Click a star to focus on it, click a second star to measure the distance between them, then click the far end of the line to travel there. Search for “Sol” to return home to our sun. Enjoy!

About Starfield

Starfield is a navigable 3D model of stars and nebulae mostly within ~1250 parsecs of the Sun. Each star is rendered at the colour and apparent brightness you would actually see from any given point in space, including the effects of light extinction through dust in the interstellar medium. Known variable stars are shown to pulsate at their catalogue period.

The catalogue is based entirely on scientific measurement: ~313,000 stars from AT-HYG v3.3, cross-matched with the GCVS variable-star catalogue. Constellation stick-figures, molecular cloud structure, and the Milky Way's volumetric backdrop are all drawn from published astronomy data. See Credits for the full list and more details.

Created by Alex Marshall and released under AGPL-3.0.
