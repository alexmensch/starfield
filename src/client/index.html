Welcome to Stellata

Stellata is an interactive 3D map of — real stars within ~1250 parsecs of the Sun. Each star is rendered at the colour and apparent brightness you would actually see from the camera's current position, including dimming and reddening from interstellar dust.

Drag to orbit, scroll to zoom, hover for a name. Click a star to focus it, click a second to measure the distance, then click the far end to travel there. Search “Sol” to come home, or press ? for keyboard shortcuts.

About Stellata

Stellata is a navigable 3D model of stars and the local interstellar medium, mostly within ~1250 parsecs of the Sun. Each star is rendered at the colour and apparent brightness you would actually see from any given point in space, including the effects of dust extinction and reddening through the local Milky Way. Known variable stars pulsate at their catalogue period.

The catalogue is based entirely on scientific measurement: ~313,000 stars from AT-HYG v3.3, cross-matched with the GCVS variable-star catalogue and the Hipparcos CCDM list of visual binaries. The Milky Way's volumetric backdrop is drawn from published density profiles. See Credits for the full list and more details.

Two camera modes: Navigate orbits, zooms, and warps between stars; Observe stands at a chosen vantage point and lets you look around. Press M while observing to switch to a Sky Atlas– style monochrome chart with proper-name and Bayer labels. Press ? for the full keyboard reference.

Created by Alex Marshall and released under AGPL-3.0.
